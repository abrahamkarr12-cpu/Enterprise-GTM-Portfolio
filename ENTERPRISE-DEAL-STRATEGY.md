# Enterprise Deal Strategy

## Purpose

A practical framework for navigating complex B2B opportunities without turning the sales process into a rigid checklist.

## 1. Build an Account Thesis

Before outreach, develop a point of view:

- Why is this account worth focused investment?
- What business priorities or trigger events may create urgency?
- Where could the solution create measurable value?
- What assumptions need to be validated?

The thesis is not the answer. It creates better questions.

## 2. Understand the Current State

Explore systems, workflows, manual processes, business constraints, previous attempts to solve the problem, and the people affected.

## 3. Quantify the Business Problem

Move from surface-level pain to impact:

- Revenue or growth impact
- Operational cost
- Time and productivity
- Risk and compliance exposure
- Customer impact
- Opportunity cost

## 4. Map the Buying Group

Identify operational users, technical evaluators, champions, blockers, procurement, legal, executive sponsors, and the economic buyer.

## 5. Apply MEDDPICC

| Area | Question |
|---|---|
| Metrics | What measurable outcome makes change worthwhile? |
| Economic Buyer | Who owns the outcome and investment decision? |
| Decision Criteria | How will alternatives be evaluated? |
| Decision Process | How will the organization reach a decision? |
| Paper Process | What contracting, legal, security, and procurement steps remain? |
| Identify Pain | What meaningful problem creates urgency? |
| Champion | Who benefits from change and will advocate internally? |
| Competition | What alternatives, including no decision, are being considered? |

## 6. Connect Solution to Business Value

Position capabilities in the context of the customer’s priorities rather than delivering a generic feature presentation.

## 7. Create Mutual Momentum

Every meaningful interaction should conclude with clear actions, owners, and timing.

## Opportunity Review

Before considering an enterprise opportunity well-qualified, I want evidence of meaningful business impact, access to relevant stakeholders, a credible decision path, and mutual commitment to moving forward.
