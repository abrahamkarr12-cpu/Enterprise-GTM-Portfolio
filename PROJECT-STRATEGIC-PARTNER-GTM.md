# Project: Strategic Partner GTM Plan

> **Portfolio scenario:** A fictionalized example demonstrating how I would build a joint GTM motion between an enterprise SaaS company and a complementary technology or services partner.

## Scenario

An enterprise SaaS company wants to grow within BFSI by developing a strategic partnership with an organization that has complementary capabilities, established customer relationships, and access to target accounts.

The goal is not simply to sign a partnership agreement. The goal is to create a repeatable motion that generates customer value and measurable pipeline.

## 1. Partnership Thesis

### Strategic Question

Can the two organizations solve a meaningful customer problem better together than independently?

### Evaluation Criteria

| Area | Question |
|---|---|
| Customer Fit | Do we serve similar buyers or accounts? |
| Capability Fit | Are the offerings complementary? |
| Market Access | Can each company open opportunities for the other? |
| Customer Value | Is the combined outcome meaningful? |
| Executive Support | Is leadership committed? |
| Field Readiness | Will sales teams participate? |
| Operational Fit | Can both organizations support the motion? |

## 2. Ideal Partner Profile

The strongest potential partner would have:

- Enterprise BFSI relationships
- Complementary technology or services
- Executive commitment
- Active field sales organization
- Clear customer use cases
- Ability to support co-selling
- Minimal direct competitive conflict

## 3. Joint Value Proposition

### Customer Problem

Enterprise financial-services organizations often operate across fragmented technology environments and complex workflows.

### Combined Value

The partnership should create a stronger outcome by combining complementary capabilities, expertise, implementation support, market access, or technology.

### Message

**Together, we help enterprise customers reduce complexity, improve operational outcomes, and accelerate transformation through a more connected solution and delivery model.**

## 5. Account Mapping

### Process

1. Agree on ICP.
2. Identify target-account overlap using approved systems and processes.
3. Map existing relationships.
4. Identify active opportunities.
5. Find whitespace.
6. Score accounts.
7. Assign owners.
8. Develop joint account hypotheses.
9. Coordinate engagement.

### Account Prioritization

| Factor | Weight |
|---|---:|
| ICP Fit | 25% |
| Existing Relationship | 20% |
| Business Trigger | 20% |
| Revenue Potential | 15% |
| Executive Access | 10% |
| Competitive Position | 10% |

## 6. GTM Motion

### Co-Sell

Both organizations identify, develop, and pursue opportunities collaboratively.

### Referral

Each partner introduces qualified opportunities where the other creates customer value.

### Joint Account Development

Teams coordinate strategy around priority accounts.

### Executive Engagement

Leadership supports strategic customer conversations and removes internal barriers.

## 7. Field Enablement

Sales teams need:

- Joint value proposition
- Ideal customer profile
- Qualification criteria
- Customer use cases
- Discovery questions
- Competitive positioning
- Rules of engagement
- Partner contacts
- Opportunity registration process

## 8. 30-60-90 Day Activation Plan

### Days 1–30: Align

- Confirm executive sponsors
- Finalize partnership thesis
- Define ICP
- Build joint value proposition
- Establish operating cadence
- Identify initial account universe

### Days 31–60: Activate

- Complete account mapping
- Select priority accounts
- Enable field teams
- Develop joint plays
- Launch coordinated outreach
- Begin opportunity reviews

### Days 61–90: Scale

- Review pipeline creation
- Analyze field participation
- Identify friction
- Capture early wins
- Refine messaging
- Expand successful plays

## 9. Operating Cadence

| Meeting | Participants | Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| Executive Review | Executive Sponsors | Quarterly |
| Alliance Review | Partnership Leaders | Monthly |
| Pipeline Review | Alliance + Sales | Biweekly |
| Account Strategy | Account Teams | As Needed |

## 10. KPIs

### Leading Indicators

- Sellers enabled
- Accounts mapped
- Joint meetings
- Opportunities identified
- Active co-sell motions

### Business Outcomes

- Partner-sourced pipeline
- Partner-influenced pipeline
- Opportunity progression
- Win rate
- Revenue
- Average deal size
- Sales-cycle impact

## Takeaway

The difference between a partnership and a productive ecosystem relationship is execution. My focus is connecting strategy to field activity, customer value, pipeline, and measurable outcomes.
