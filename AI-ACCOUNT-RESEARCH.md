# AI-Powered Account Research Workflow

## Objective

Use AI to improve preparation and strategic thinking—not replace human judgment, customer discovery, or fact verification.

## Step 1: Gather Reliable Inputs

Research approved public sources such as company materials, executive commentary, earnings information when applicable, industry developments, leadership changes, and relevant news.

## Step 2: Identify Business Priorities

Use AI to organize information around:

- Strategic initiatives
- Growth priorities
- Operational pressures
- Industry challenges
- Technology changes
- Trigger events

## Step 3: Develop Account Hypotheses

Create hypotheses about potential problems, stakeholders, business impact, and areas where the solution may be relevant.

A hypothesis is something to validate—not something to present as fact.

## Step 4: Map Potential Stakeholders

Identify likely business leaders, operational users, technical stakeholders, financial decision makers, and executive sponsors.

## Step 5: Prepare Better Discovery

Use the research to develop questions that cannot be answered through basic public research.

Examples:

- How is the organization currently measuring the impact of this issue?
- Which teams experience the greatest operational friction?
- What changed internally that made this a priority now?
- How would leadership evaluate whether an investment was successful?

## Step 6: Build a Pre-Call Brief

Create a concise output covering:

1. Company priorities
2. Relevant trigger events
3. Stakeholder hypotheses
4. Potential business problems
5. Value themes
6. Risks and unknowns
7. Discovery questions
8. Recommended next-best actions

## Step 7: Verify

Validate important claims against reliable sources before using them in customer conversations or decision making.

## Principles

- AI output is a starting point, not verified truth.
- Sensitive information belongs only in approved systems.
- Better research should create better conversations, not robotic scripts.
- The goal is to spend less time gathering information and more time applying judgment.
