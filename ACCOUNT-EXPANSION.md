# Enterprise Account Expansion Plan

## My View

Account growth begins with customer value. Expansion is more sustainable when it solves a real business problem rather than simply pushing additional products.

## 1. Understand the Business Context

Track customer priorities, industry pressures, leadership changes, growth initiatives, acquisitions, regulatory changes, and other developments.

## 2. Measure Value Realization

Understand:

- What outcomes were originally expected?
- What value has been delivered?
- Where is value unclear or unrealized?
- What has changed since the original purchase?

## 3. Build Relationship Coverage

Map users, champions, executive sponsors, technical stakeholders, decision makers, detractors, and areas where the relationship depends too heavily on one person.

## 4. Identify Risk

Evaluate adoption, satisfaction, support issues, organizational change, competitive threats, budget pressure, and gaps between expected and realized value.

## 5. Map Whitespace

Explore additional teams, business units, geographies, use cases, products, and problems that may create legitimate expansion opportunities.

## 6. Create an Expansion Hypothesis

Define the problem, affected stakeholders, potential value, required relationships, internal resources, and questions that need validation.

## 7. Use Executive Business Reviews Strategically

An EBR should:

1. Reconfirm business priorities.
2. Review agreed goals and outcomes.
3. Discuss progress and value.
4. Surface risks and changes.
5. Explore upcoming priorities.
6. Align on additional opportunities.
7. Agree on actions, owners, and timing.

## 8. Apply New-Logo Discipline

Treat meaningful expansion opportunities with the same qualification, stakeholder mapping, business-case development, and mutual action planning used in new enterprise sales.
