# Project: AI-Powered Enterprise Account Intelligence

> **Portfolio scenario:** A practical example of how I use AI as a research and strategy accelerator while keeping verification, business judgment, and human relationships at the center of enterprise selling.

## Business Problem

Enterprise sellers often spend hours moving between websites, reports, news, CRM data, notes, and internal resources before an important customer conversation.

The challenge is not a lack of information. It is turning information into useful insight.

## Objective

Create a repeatable AI-assisted workflow that transforms approved public information into:

- Account context
- Strategic priorities
- Trigger events
- Stakeholder hypotheses
- Potential business problems
- Value hypotheses
- Discovery questions
- Recommended next actions

## Workflow

### Step 1: Define the Business Question

Poor question:

> Tell me about this company.

Better question:

> What recent strategic, operational, financial, or leadership developments could affect this organization’s priorities over the next 12 months?

AI is more useful when the business objective is clear.

## Step 2: Gather Reliable Inputs

Potential sources include:

- Company website
- Annual reports
- Investor materials
- Earnings commentary
- Executive interviews
- Press releases
- Leadership changes
- Relevant industry news
- Regulatory developments

Important information should be verified against reliable sources.

## Step 3: Structure the Research

I organize information into six categories:

| Category | What I’m Looking For |
|---|---|
| Strategy | Growth and transformation priorities |
| Financial | Performance pressures and investments |
| Operational | Friction, complexity, and efficiency |
| Technology | Modernization and platform initiatives |
| People | Leadership and organizational changes |
| Market | Competition, regulation, and industry trends |

## Step 4: Separate Facts from Hypotheses

### Fact

A statement supported by a reliable source.

### Hypothesis

A potential implication that needs validation.

Example:

**Fact:** The company announced an acquisition.

**Hypothesis:** Integrating systems, data, and workflows may become a priority.

That distinction is critical. AI should help create better questions—not manufacture certainty.

## Step 5: Develop a Stakeholder Map

For each potential stakeholder:

- What do they likely own?
- What business outcome matters to them?
- How could the problem affect their organization?
- What questions should be asked?
- Who else may influence the decision?

## Step 6: Create a Value Hypothesis

Use this structure:

> Based on **[verified business development]**, the organization may be experiencing **[potential challenge]**, which could affect **[business outcome]**. I would validate this by speaking with **[stakeholders]** and exploring **[discovery areas]**.

## Step 7: Generate Role-Specific Discovery Questions

### CFO

- How is leadership evaluating the financial impact of the current environment?
- Where does operational complexity create avoidable cost or risk?

### CIO / CTO

- Which modernization priorities are most important over the next 12–24 months?
- Where does the current technology environment create scalability or integration challenges?

### Operations

- Which workflows require the most manual intervention?
- Where do delays or inconsistencies have the greatest downstream impact?

## Step 8: Produce the Executive Brief

### Account

Fictional Enterprise Financial Services Company

### Strategic Context

### Potential Trigger Events

- Leadership changes
- Modernization initiatives
- Regulatory developments

### Stakeholder Hypotheses

- CIO / CTO
- CFO

- Operations

### Potential Business Problems

- Fragmented systems
- Manual workflows

- Slow reporting

### Value Themes

- Faster decision making

### Unknowns to Validate

2. Identify relevant stakeholders.

4. Validate hypotheses through discovery.
5. Update the account strategy based on customer evidence.

## Example Prompt Architecture

```text
ROLE
Act as an enterprise account strategist.

OBJECTIVE
Analyze the provided verified source material and help me prepare for a customer conversation.

RULES
- Separate verified facts from hypotheses.
- Do not invent company information.
- Identify missing information.
- Explain the business relevance of each insight.
- Create questions that validate assumptions.

OUTPUT
1. Executive summary
2. Strategic priorities
3. Trigger events
4. Stakeholder hypotheses
5. Potential business problems
6. Value hypotheses
7. Risks and unknowns
8. Discovery questions
9. Recommended next actions
```

## Human Review Checklist

Before using the output:

- Are important claims verified?
- Are facts clearly separated from inference?
- Does the research connect to a real business objective?
- Are the questions specific?
- Is any sensitive information being handled improperly?
- Would this preparation improve the customer conversation?

## Takeaway

The value of AI in enterprise GTM is not generating more content. It is helping professionals move faster from information to insight—while preserving verification, judgment, and authentic human engagement.
