# Strategic Partnership GTM Plan

## My View

A partnership is not successful because two companies sign an agreement or publish a press release. It succeeds when the relationship creates measurable value for customers and both organizations.

## 1. Evaluate Strategic Fit

Assess customer overlap, complementary capabilities, market access, competitive conflicts, executive commitment, field engagement, and operational readiness.

## 2. Define the Joint Customer Problem

Start with the customer:

- What problem can we solve better together?
- Why does the combined value proposition matter?
- Which customers and use cases are the strongest fit?

## 3. Build the Joint Value Proposition

Create a simple explanation of the customer problem, combined value, target buyer, business outcome, and why the partnership matters now.

## 4. Identify the GTM Motion

Determine whether the relationship is primarily referral, co-sell, reseller, services, integration, marketplace, or a combination.

## 5. Map Accounts

1. Align on ICP and target market.
2. Identify customer and prospect overlap using approved processes.
3. Find whitespace and existing relationships.
4. Prioritize by fit, timing, access, and value.
5. Assign relationship owners.
6. Develop joint account hypotheses.

## 6. Enable the Field

Provide concise messaging, qualification criteria, use cases, engagement rules, and a clear process for bringing the partner into opportunities.

## 7. Establish Operating Cadence

Create consistent communication between executives, alliance owners, sales teams, and other stakeholders.

## 8. Measure What Matters

Track activated partners, account engagement, sourced pipeline, influenced pipeline, opportunity progression, revenue, wins and losses, and field participation.

## Guiding Principle

Partnership strategy should connect ecosystem activity to customer value and measurable business outcomes.
