# Project: Enterprise Account Plan

> **Portfolio scenario:** A fictionalized example created to demonstrate how I approach complex enterprise account strategy. No confidential customer or employer information is used.

## Scenario

A national financial-services company is evaluating ways to modernize fragmented data and workflow processes across multiple business units. The organization has grown through acquisition, creating inconsistent systems, manual reporting, duplicated work, and limited executive visibility.

The opportunity involves a 6–12 month enterprise sales cycle, multiple stakeholders, technical validation, security review, procurement, and executive approval.

## 1. Account Thesis

The strongest opportunity is not to lead with software capabilities. It is to connect fragmented processes to measurable business impact.

### Business Hypothesis

The organization may be experiencing:

- High operational costs caused by manual workflows
- Inconsistent data across business units
- Slow reporting and decision making
- Increased compliance and governance risk
- Difficulty scaling existing processes
- Limited executive visibility into performance

### Why Change?

The status quo becomes increasingly expensive as the organization grows. The business case for change should focus on efficiency, risk reduction, scalability, and better decision making.

## 2. Research Plan

Before outreach, I would research:

- Annual reports and investor materials
- Executive priorities
- M&A activity
- Leadership changes
- Technology initiatives
- Regulatory pressures
- Hiring patterns
- Relevant industry developments

The goal is to develop informed hypotheses—not assume I understand the customer before discovery.

## 3. Stakeholder Map

| Stakeholder | Likely Priority | Engagement Strategy |
|---|---|---|
| CIO / CTO | Architecture, security, scalability | Connect modernization to enterprise strategy |
| CFO | ROI, cost, risk | Quantify business impact and cost of inaction |
| Business Unit Leader | Performance and outcomes | Understand operational priorities |
| Operations Leader | Workflow efficiency | Identify friction and manual processes |
| IT / Security | Integration and risk | Engage early and build technical confidence |
| Procurement | Commercial terms | Understand process before late-stage negotiation |
| Executive Sponsor | Strategic outcome | Maintain alignment throughout the deal |
| Champion | Internal change | Equip with business case and internal messaging |

## 4. Discovery Strategy

### Current State

- How are these processes managed today?
- Where does information move between systems or teams manually?
- Which parts of the workflow create the most friction?
- What has the organization already tried?

### Business Impact

- How does the current environment affect cost, productivity, risk, or growth?
- Which teams are most affected?
- How is leadership measuring the problem today?
- What happens if the organization maintains the current approach for another 12–24 months?

### Decision

- What would need to be true for the organization to make a change?
- Who needs to be involved?
- How will alternatives be evaluated?
- What internal processes could affect timing?

## 5. MEDDPICC View

| Element | Validation Goal |
|---|---|
| Metrics | Quantified efficiency, revenue, cost, or risk outcome |
| Economic Buyer | Access to executive who owns outcome and investment |
| Decision Criteria | Clear technical and business evaluation requirements |
| Decision Process | Documented path from evaluation to approval |
| Paper Process | Security, legal, procurement, and contracting mapped |
| Pain | Meaningful problem with organizational impact |
| Champion | Influential stakeholder invested in change |
| Competition | Vendors, internal solutions, and status quo understood |

## 6. Opportunity Strategy

### Phase 1: Learn
Research, discovery, stakeholder mapping, and qualification.

### Phase 2: Align
Build consensus around the problem, desired outcomes, and evaluation criteria.

### Phase 3: Prove
Demonstrate relevant value, complete technical validation, and develop the business case.

### Phase 4: Navigate
Manage executive alignment, procurement, security, legal, and commercial negotiations.

### Phase 5: Commit
Agree on implementation expectations, success measures, ownership, and the path forward.

## 8. Success Measures

I would evaluate execution through:

- Stakeholder coverage
- Executive access
- Champion strength
- Business impact quantified
- Decision process clarity
- Technical validation completed
- Mutual next steps maintained
- Forecast confidence
- Deal progression

## Takeaway

My objective in a complex enterprise opportunity is to create clarity: clarity around the problem, business impact, stakeholders, decision process, and value of change.
